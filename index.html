Our Love Story

The Story Begins

Dec 4, 2009

Anthony and Victoria grew up in the same boring small town of Hawthorne, NY. Unless beauty salons, pizzerias, and banks are your jam, not much really goes down in H-Town. So, a small boring town filled with marinara sauce and too much hairspray and fake tanner - not really the perfect starting off point for a love story (unless you consider The Nanny the love story of the century). So then, what happened? How did two awkward kids who went to school together since the first grade wade through all of the gabagool and fake nails to find each other? Well the answer is simple: I don't know. But, it happened.

It wasn't until junior year of high school where these two love birds met through mutual friends and finally mustered up the courage to talk to each other. So what does a group of 16 - 17 year olds do in such a vibrant and lively town such as Hawthorne on a Saturday night? They go to the local shopping center of course. Ah yes, Rose Hill: the epicenter of romance for any high school kid at Westlake. They even have a pizzeria, a beauty salon, and a bank all in the same shopping center so you don't have to drive 30 seconds to the other nearest one.

This went on a few more months until Anthony decided that he needed to make this girl his. So on December 4th, 2009 - a day after Victoria's birthday. Anthony (finally) asked out the girl he knew he was going to marry one day and she would soon become Victoria Mascia.

The Proposal

June 27, 2018

Months and months of planning went in to this night. Every single minuscule detail that Anthony could think of from location, to time of night, to cloud coverage, to planning the route from the restaurant, to what freaking knee to go down on, he thought of. He was like one of those neurotic women you see on those trashy "fiancé" reality shows on TLC. He had a whole speech he had planned out that he was going to tell her before he got down on his knee. He even rehearsed it like a middle school kid going out for their first play audition. But, as soon as Anthony got in to that candle heart, he forgot every single word that he was going to say. Now you're thinking "aw he was so nervous yaass so kyoot" (direct quote from Victoria after the fact) - but he wasn't. He was calm for the first time in months - no more anxiety, no more racing thoughts, everything he was so worried about was completely forgotten... because none of that mattered anymore. In that moment, not only did Anthony see his future wife, but he saw a lifetime of happiness. In what felt like 4 hours (which was actually probably only 3 seconds), he saw a marriage, a first home, a first child, a first grandchild, retirement, and living out the rest of their days just them two... just the way it started. Because in the end, when the dust settles and the craziness of life stops, even if momentarily... it will always be her standing there right next to him where she will finally become Mrs. Victoria Mascia!

Dear friends and family, As my wife and I sit down to reflect on our wedding day, we cannot express enough gratitude for each and every one of you who came to celebrate with us. The day was nothing short of magical, and we are so honored that you were there to share it with us. From the moment we exchanged our vows, to the moment we hit the dance floor, we felt surrounded by an overwhelming amount of love and support. It was truly a day that we will never forget, and we owe it all to you. Your presence, your energy, and your love made our day truly special. We also want to extend a special thank you to our parents, who have supported us throughout our lives and have helped us become the people we are today. As we move forward in our new journey together as husband and wife, we know that we will always carry the memories of our wedding day with us. And we will always be grateful for each and every one of you who helped make it possible. Thank you again for sharing in this special moment with us. Sincerely, Anthony and Victoria Mascia
